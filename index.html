Learning Objectives & Bloom's Taxonomy

Cognitive Levels Applied: Knowledge (Define EI components) ‚Üí Application (Apply to scenarios) ‚Üí Evaluation (Assess leadership responses). This progression ensures deep learning and practical skill transfer.

Constructivist Learning Theory

Active Knowledge Building: Learners click to reveal concepts, make scenario choices, and receive personalized feedback. This hands-on approach helps them construct understanding rather than passively consume content.

Gamification & Engagement

Motivation Elements: Progress tracking, personalized greetings, achievement celebration, and interactive elements maintain engagement. The 5-minute format respects busy schedules while maximizing retention.

Experiential Learning (Kolb's Cycle)

Complete Learning Loop: Concrete Experience (scenario) ‚Üí Reflective Observation (feedback) ‚Üí Abstract Conceptualization (theory) ‚Üí Active Experimentation (next steps). This ensures practical application.

Learning Design Principles Applied

  • Microlearning: 5-minute format increases completion rates and fits busy schedules
  • Personalization: Name integration creates emotional connection and ownership
  • Immediate Feedback: Real-time responses reinforce correct behaviors and correct misconceptions
  • Scenario-Based Learning: Realistic workplace situations ensure practical relevance
  • Progressive Disclosure: Information revealed step-by-step prevents cognitive overload
  • Social Learning Theory: Modeling correct EI behaviors through scenario examples
  • Spaced Repetition: Key concepts reinforced across multiple screens and formats

Technical Implementation

LMS Compatibility: This design can be implemented in Rise 360, Storyline, Captivate, or any SCORM-compliant LMS. The interactive elements translate well to authoring tools, and the data tracking can integrate with xAPI/Tin Can API for detailed analytics.

Key Takeaways for You

  • Self-Awareness is the foundation of emotional intelligence. Regular reflection on your emotions and triggers improves decision-making.
  • Self-Management helps you stay composed under pressure and adapt to change, modeling resilience for your team.
  • Social Awareness enables you to read the room, understand team dynamics, and respond with empathy.
  • Relationship Management transforms awareness into action through effective communication, conflict resolution, and team building.
  • In challenging situations, pause to acknowledge emotions before problem-solving. This validates concerns and builds trust.

Next Steps for You: Practice one EI skill this week. Start with self-awareness by journaling about your emotional responses to workplace situations. Notice patterns and consider how they impact your leadership effectiveness.
